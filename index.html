Vocalmeet
CRM Redesign

Full redesign of a SaaS CRM platform — scalable Design System, radical user flow optimisation and a modern cohesive UI from discovery to handoff.

UX/UI Design, Prototyping, Design System, Handoff
Figma
2025

Overview

The challenge

The Vocalmeet platform had long, fragmented flows creating unnecessary friction and slowing down task completion. Navigation between sections felt cumbersome — users needed many clicks to reach their goals. The interface was also outdated and inconsistent due to the absence of a unified Design System.

My objective was to streamline the experience by reducing steps, optimising navigation and introducing a modern, cohesive UI built on a scalable, reusable design foundation.

Process

How it was solved

01 · Discovery

In-depth conversations with the CEO and development team. Benchmarks against Stripe, Pipedrive and Relevance AI to define guiding UX principles.

02 · Definition

Mapped critical user flows and set clear goals: consolidate two sidebars into one, visual redesign, expanded data fields and click reduction.

03 · Design System

Complete Design System in Figma with reusable components, design tokens and detailed documentation to align design with development.

04 · Prototyping & Handoff

High-fidelity designs and interactive prototypes tested with stakeholders, delivered with full implementation documentation for the dev team.

Visual Impact

Before & after

Screenshots from the Vocalmeet CRM highlight improvements in layout, colour usage and simplified flows. Key interactions now allow faster task completion with less cognitive load.

CRM screens — redesigned navigation, contacts and dashboard views

Design System

A scalable foundation

The Design System was built in Figma from scratch, covering colour tokens, typography scale, spacing system, component library and interaction documentation — ensuring every future screen stays consistent.

Contacts & dashboard

Inspection App

Mobile application digitising vehicle and equipment mobilisation checklists at Vale — replacing paper forms with an offline-ready, MEV-integrated solution.

User Flow, UI/UX Design, Mobile
Figma
2024

Overview

The challenge

The Vale App Checklist project was designed to digitise and streamline the vehicle and equipment mobilisation checklist process. The current process was entirely manual — printed forms filled out in the field, scanned and sent via email — creating data integrity issues and delays.

The goal was to create a mobile application enabling digital checklists, even offline, storing data systematically and integrating with the MEV system used across Vale's operations.

Vale App Checklist — mobile interface for field operations

Features

Built for the field

Digital Checklists

Replaced manual paper forms with structured digital checklists — faster, consistent and always accessible on mobile devices.

Offline Mode

Designed for remote industrial environments without internet access. Data is stored locally and synchronised with MEV when back online.

MEV Integration

Seamless data synchronisation with Vale's internal MEV management system, eliminating manual re-entry and reducing errors.

Accessibility First

Interface optimised for field workers — larger touch targets, high contrast, and usability validated for older mobile devices used in industrial environments.

My contributions

Design decisions

  • User Flow & Wireframing — Designed streamlined flows to simplify data entry and validation for field workers
  • Mobile-first UI — Interface built for industrial conditions: glare, gloves, time pressure and older devices
  • Offline-first architecture — UX patterns that work seamlessly regardless of connectivity
  • Accessibility — WCAG-aligned touch targets, contrast ratios and navigation patterns

Results

Measurable impact

0Paper forms — fully digital process
✓Works offline in remote areas
↑Data accuracy & operational efficiency

By implementing these strategies, the manual, error-prone process was transformed into a digital, efficient and scalable solution — improving operational efficiency and data integrity across Vale's field operations.

EBAC Games →

© 2025 Rodrigo Cafiero.Porto, Portugal. Worldwide
Gaming · Mobile · UX Design · 2022

EBAC Games —
Mobile Gaming

Digital platform giving visibility to indie mobile game studios — intuitive discovery, organised catalogue and a smooth purchase flow for players and creators.

UX Design, Research, Prototyping
Figma
2022

Overview

About the project

EBAC Games is a digital platform focused on indie mobile games, with the mission of giving visibility to small studios and independent developers — promoting unique and creative experiences in the gaming world.

The main goal was to design an intuitive, accessible and engaging digital experience that supports both players and game creators. We developed a website that emphasises usability, a well-organised game catalogue and a smooth, secure purchasing flow.

EBAC Games — platform overview showing game catalogue and creator tools

Process

Research to design

Through research, benchmarking, user personas and usability testing, we structured a user journey that prioritises the discovery, promotion and monetisation of indie games. Developers can manage their content with ease, while users enjoy a seamless browsing experience.

Research & Benchmarking

Competitive analysis of leading game platforms (Steam, itch.io, Apple Arcade). User personas for both players (discovery-focused) and creators (visibility and monetisation).

User Journey Mapping

Structured dual journeys: players browsing and purchasing games, and developers managing and monetising their content — both needing clear, different paths.

Usability Testing

Tested key flows — game discovery, purchase and developer content management — iterating based on real user feedback until smooth and intuitive.

Final Design

Visual design balancing the playful aesthetic of gaming culture with the clarity needed for a commerce and creator platform.

Key design decisions

Two users, one platform

  • Dual user architecture — clear, separate flows for players (browsing, discovery, purchase) and developers (upload, manage, analyse)
  • Game catalogue design — organised browsing with filters, categories and featured indie titles to maximise discoverability
  • Purchase flow — simplified checkout with clear pricing, secure payment indicators and instant download access
  • Developer dashboard — intuitive content management with upload tools, analytics and promotional options
  • Mobile-first approach — optimised for the primary device where indie mobile games are played and discovered

Results

Measurable impact

2User types served — players and creators
✓Purchase flow validated in usability testing
↑Indie game discoverability and creator tools
